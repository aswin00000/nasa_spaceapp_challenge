Eclipse Visualization

What is a solar eclipse—and how often do they happen? A solar eclipse occurs when a new moon aligns perfectly between Earth and the sun and briefly casts a shadow on our planet, called an umbra. The moon's diameter and distance from Earth make its relative size just big enough to cover the sun's disk—either partially or fully, depending on the distances between the three celestial bodies.
